The History of the NHL: From Origins to Today

  • Post author By raulhuja
  • Post date April 1, 2025
  • Categories In Uncategorized

(Image of NHL in the 1917)

The National Hockey League (NHL) has come a long way since its founding in 1917. Originally consisting of just four teams, the league has expanded to 32 franchises across North America. Over the decades, the NHL has seen legendary players such as Wayne Gretzky, Mario Lemieux, and Sidney Crosby shape the game. From the Original Six era to modern expansions, hockey continues to grow in popularity.

Key Milestones in NHL History:

  • 1917 – NHL founded with four teams: Montreal Canadiens, Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, and Toronto Arenas.
  • 1942-1967 – The “Original Six” era featuring teams like the Maple Leafs, Bruins, and Blackhawks.
  • 1967 Expansion – The NHL doubles in size to 12 teams.
  • 1979 – Merger with the World Hockey Association (WHA) brings teams like the Edmonton Oilers into the NHL.
  • 2005 – The introduction of the salary cap following the lockout.
  • 2021 – The Seattle Kraken joins the league as the 32nd team.

With a rich history of thrilling moments and intense rivalries, the NHL remains one of the most exciting sports leagues in the world.
